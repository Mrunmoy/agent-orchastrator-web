Chapter: The Domain Awakens

Saga Chapter · 2026-03-07 · ORCH-001

Before a kingdom can wage war, it must know the names of things.

The builders had raised walls and watchtowers. They had carved gateways into the stone and taught sentinels to read the wind. But the great hall stood empty, because no one had yet spoken the True Names — the words that would give shape to every creature, every quest, every artifact that would pass through its doors.

And so the Namer descended into the deep library beneath the fortress. By torchlight, they unrolled the ancient specification scrolls — nine tablets of enumerated truths. Provider, the scroll declared: four bloodlines of agents that could be summoned. AgentRole: worker, coordinator, moderator — the trinity of purpose. ConversationState: seven stages from the first spark of debate to the final silence of completion or failure. Phase: six gates through which every quest must pass, from the opening argument to the final merge. And more — GateStatus, TaskStatus, RunStatus, ArtifactType — each one a compass rose pointing to states that had, until now, existed only in whispered intent.

With the names inscribed, the Namer began to shape the vessels. Eight forms emerged from the naming clay: the Agent, who carries a provider's mark and knows its own capabilities; the Conversation, a long-lived workspace that remembers its phase, its gate, and the ghost of its last summary; the ConversationAgent, the binding pact that seats an agent at a conversation's table in a specific order with specific permissions; the Task, a quest card that tracks dependencies and evidence; the Artifact, the crystallized output of agreement or conflict; the MessageEvent, each word spoken in the great hall preserved with its timestamp and origin; the SchedulerRun, the drum beat that drives agents through their batched turns; and the ResourceSnapshot, the sentinel's reading of the machine's vital signs.

The Namer tested each vessel before declaring it ready. Forty-three trials — every enum checked for its full roster of members, every vessel filled with valid contents and inspected for soundness, every optional seal verified to rest at nothing until deliberately set. All trials were written before the first vessel was shaped, as the law of this realm demands. Red, then green.

When the last test burned green, the Namer carried the vessels up to the great hall and placed them on the registry shelf, visible to all who would build upon them — the repository layer, the scheduler, the API, the resume builder. Each could now speak the same language, trade the same tokens, and trust that a Task in one hand was the same Task in another.

The domain had awakened. The orchestrator could begin to think.
